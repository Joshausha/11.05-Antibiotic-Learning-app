# Phase 8: Learning Analytics - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-01-07
**Status:** Ready for planning

<vision>
## How This Should Work

A dedicated analytics dashboard that's stark and practical — board prep focused. No gamification fluff, no playful streaks. Just clear, actionable insights about your learning progress.

When you open the analytics view, you see your improvement trajectory front and center. The core question it answers: "Am I actually getting better?" You should be able to see weak spots (aminoglycosides, gram negatives, etc.) and have direct paths to study them, but the primary value is understanding your progress over time.

Think UWorld-style analytics — clinical, data-dense, professional. Not a game, not social media. Just useful information about where you stand.

</vision>

<essential>
## What Must Be Nailed

- **Progress over time visualization** — The core of this phase. See your improvement trajectory clearly. Are your scores going up? Are you studying consistently? This is THE metric that matters.
- **Weak spot identification** — Secondary but important. Show where you're struggling so you can focus effort.
- **Direct action links** — From any identified weak area, one click to study/quiz on it.

</essential>

<boundaries>
## What's Out of Scope

- **No social/comparative features** — No leaderboards, no comparing to other users, no sharing progress. This is personal learning, not competition.
- **No complex spaced repetition tuning** — Algorithm configuration stays hidden. Use sensible defaults, just show results.
- **No cross-device sync** — Local storage only. No account system, no cloud sync. Keep it simple.

</boundaries>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

No specific requirements - open to standard approaches for the visualization implementation. The key constraint is the board-prep aesthetic: clean, professional, data-focused. Not playful or gamified.

</specifics>

<notes>
## Additional Context

This phase builds on the quiz system (Phase 4) to track performance data. The analytics should feel like a natural extension of the board-prep quiz experience — same professional, practical tone.

Priority is the progress trajectory. Weak spots and recommendations are valuable but secondary to showing "am I improving?"

</notes>
